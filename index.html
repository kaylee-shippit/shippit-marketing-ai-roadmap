The endgame
Remove the low-leverage work between functions.
The business will ask: "How is Marketing getting more efficient with AI?" The answer isn't a list of tools — it's a system where AI handles the hand-offs: the reformatting, the starting-from-scratch, the manual coordination between Research, Content, and Distribution. Every function redeploys to the work that actually moves pipeline.
The opportunity ahead: the team, the tools, and the proof points already exist. What this plan does is connect them — so Marketing can produce more, respond faster, and spend more time on the work that requires genuine human judgment.
✓ Already proven with Delivered Primary research → content → multi-channel distribution → signal capture. Running in a 2-week cycle, without AI. This system scales and automates that model across all of marketing.

Research & Positioning
Sofia · Kathy · DRI: Sofia Nilsson Burston
How it works today
Research lives in Confluence decks and slides. Turning positioning into battle cards, one-pagers, or objection handlers requires coordinating across multiple people each time — a time-consuming process that can slow down the sales team.
With AI: Self-serve enablement, always fresh
Structured Confluence template
→
Airtable structured data layer
→
Gamma + gen AI generate assets
Airtable sits between Confluence and generation — it's the structured, queryable data source that gives Gamma (and other tools) clean inputs for decks, PDFs, imagery, and slides. The same Airtable records hold QA scores; PMM reviews and scores quarterly until the system can self-assess. Endgame: anyone in the business generates their own collateral on demand — PMM stops being a bottleneck for every asset, and content stays fresh because a new version is a button press away.

What you get back
Sofia and Kathy's time shifts toward primary research, relationships, and market sensing — the work no AI can replace. The system handles individual asset production; PMM sets the standard it runs on.

Distribution Engine
Kaylee · Christina · DRI: Kaylee Liu
How it works today
Campaign briefs written from scratch each time. Messaging adapted manually per segment. Tasks and goals set informally, with coordination spread across Slack, email, and ClickUp — making it hard to maintain a clear picture from brief to launch.
With AI: From inputs to execution automatically
PMM positioning + Content pipeline
→
AI drafts campaign plan + channel variants
→
Kaylee QA + approves
→
System auto-creates ClickUp tasks + assigns metrics from OKRs
The brief is generated from PMM and Content inputs — not from scratch. Once approved, the system creates ClickUp tasks with the right deadlines, assigns owners, and defines success metrics directly from the team's OKRs. Stretch goal: campaign targeting maps to the sales territory database — so every campaign knows which accounts it's expected to reach, without Kaylee manually cross-referencing. Christina (organic social + ad-hoc email) is part of this engine — her channels connect to the same brief and pipeline.
ClickUp ✓ Webflow ✓ LinkedIn Ads ✓ OKR asset — owner: Kaylee, due Apr 21
What you get back
Kaylee and Christina move from execution coordinators to strategists. The system handles the repetitive build — they focus on what's working, why, and what to do next. Their value is judgment, not task management.

The three-layer system
Each layer feeds the next. AI handles the hand-offs between them.

Primary research is the core — it's what makes everything else credible. Content turns that research into assets. Distribution gets those assets to the right people at the right time.

AI amplifies the output of each layer and automates the hand-offs between them. The result: a team that's more connected to the market — because primary research actually happens — and producing more output, because the administrative work is handled by the system.
